Function vectors are
two populations.

FV heads in language models don't form one functional class. They split into writers that push the rule-correct logit up and cancellers that push it down — invisible to magnitude-only ranking.

01  ·  The setup

One number per head isn't enough.

In a language model doing in-context rule learning, a handful of attention heads carry most of the causal signal toward the correct label. The standard practice — going back to todd et al. 2024 — is to rank these function-vector heads by the magnitude of their causal effect and treat the top-K as one functional class.

Implicit in that recipe is a sign assumption: every high-magnitude head pushes the model toward the rule. We show this assumption fails empirically. Replacing the magnitude-only ranking with a sign-preserving one (refined direct logit attribution + permutation FDR) and then validating each candidate with path patching, the FV head set splits.

02  ·  The discovery

Writers push up. Cancellers push down.

For each (model, task) cell we form prompt pairs that share the query but differ in rule, and measure each candidate head's direct contribution to the per-pair swing in log p(correct) − log p(incorrect). Heads with a positive direct effect of ≥ 5% are writers; ≤ −5% are cancellers. The split is robust to a ±50% perturbation of either gate.

writer vs canceller on the same readout

Both heads sit in the same model and the same prompt — only their direct effect on the readout differs.

Within a single FV-candidate set, two opposing populations co-exist at the same readout.

03  ·  The pattern

Ablate W  ⇒  logit drops. Ablate C  ⇒  logit climbs.

For each of six main cells we group-ablate the writers, the cancellers, and their union, and measure the shift in log p(correct) − log p(incorrect) with paired-prompt BCa 95% CIs. The four-condition canonical pattern — opposite signs, both subgroups above 0.10 nats with CIs excluding zero, and an attenuated joint — holds in 6 / 6 cells under zero-strategy ablation, and 11 / 12 outcomes across two strategies.

6 main cells · BCa 95% CI ablate W (red), ablate C (teal), joint (grey)

Canonical verdict holds in all 6 cells under zero-strategy ablation.

A sign-shuffle null over n=10,000 draws rejects population homogeneity at family-wise α = 0.05 (Holm–Bonferroni) in 5 / 6 main cells.

04  ·  Everywhere

Three families. Six Pythia scales. 13 of 15 cells.

Beyond the six main Pythia cells we test the verdict on nine extension cells: Pythia 2.8B / 6.9B / 12B on both tasks, Qwen2.5-1.5B / 7B on modular, and GPT-2-medium on modular — covering a Llama-style attention family and a Conv1D-style family alongside GPT-NeoX. All 9 extension cells pass the signed-direction condition; two boundary cells (6.9B-modular, 12B-hierarchical) shift to PARTIAL because one CI grazes zero. Combined with the six main cells: 13 / 15 canonical, 2 / 15 partial, 0 / 15 sign-flipped.

15 cells · group-lesion shifts (paper App. B.5) writer ablation ↓   canceller ablation ↑   joint attenuated

The W / C structure is not a Pythia artefact — it transfers to a Llama-style architecture and to GPT-2 unchanged.

05  ·  Why magnitude misses one population

The Todd top-K is task-conditionally biased.

Todd's mean-ablation top-20 — the canonical FV identification — surfaces whichever sub-population locally dominates the readout on a given task, and silently misses the other. Pooled across the six main cells:

Todd top-20 ∩ sign-aware partition · pooled across 6 main cells

Magnitude-only ranking is correct for finding heads with the largest direct effect — and exactly wrong for finding the population structure of those effects.

06  ·  Mechanism

Cancellers re-route attention from labels to format.

Five-bucket QK source decomposition splits the readout-token attention mass into BOS, format-prefix, demonstration-input, demonstration-label, and query-input. Writers concentrate on demonstration labels; cancellers shift mass toward the format-prefix tokens. The direction of the difference is consistent in 6 / 6 cells on the two load-bearing buckets.

attention mass at readout · 5 source buckets · per cell writers (red) vs cancellers (teal)

A natural follow-up: where do these heads sit? If cancellers were a late-stage rewrite mechanism, they should cluster after the writers in depth. They don't — they live in the same early-to-mid layers, and 23 / 27 sit downstream of at least one writer in the same cell. (And per-source DLA: 20 / 27 cancellers — 74% — are content-driven, not sink-driven; the seven sink heads cluster near zero.)

layer geometry · all FV-candidate heads writers (red) and cancellers (teal) by layer · size ∝ |DLA|

Dashed lines indicate the canceller has at least one writer earlier in the model — a layer-position annotation (paper's 23 / 27 claim), not a V-cascade causal link. The specific writer drawn here is the closest one below by layer.

Cancellers cluster in early-to-mid layers, not late, ruling out the "late-stage rewrite" explanation.

07  ·  A single head

L11.H4 doesn't match either named template.

The dominant canceller in both Pythia-410M cells is layer 11, head 4. We narrow its mechanism with three interventions: V-shuffle (permute the head's value vectors across source positions), an OV singular-value decomposition, and V-composition with its dominant upstream writer L10.H9. The picture that emerges fits neither rank-1 copy-suppression nor V-cascade.

V-shuffle on L11.H4 · 200 paired prompts

Permuting the head's value vectors collapses the negative DLA by ~82% on hier and ~53% on mod — the head reads content, not a fixed direction.

OV singular spectrum · WoWv

Top-1 mode carries only 2.8% of the Frobenius mass. Toggle to raw σₖ to see the spectrum is essentially flat — no rank-1 plateau.

The third intervention asks: does L11.H4's role survive a change of template? We transfer the head's identity from rule tasks to two vocabulary-ICL tasks (antonym, country-capital) and run a solo ablation on each. The role flips.

L11.H4 solo ablation · 4 templates · paired bootstrap 95% CI canceller (teal) · writer (red) · null (grey)

Same head, four templates, three roles. The "address" L11.H4 carries over; what it computes is task-conditional — the behaviour a "suppress what was demonstrated" mechanism would predict.

08  ·  What it means

Drop the cancellers, the correct answer moves up.

The population finding has an immediate, calibration-free consequence. Zero-ablating the canceller subgroup at inference time shifts the correct-incorrect log-probability difference by +0.13 to +0.29 nats in 6 / 6 main cells (paired bootstrap CI excluding 0), and produces a directionally consistent +2 to +7 pp accuracy effect (CI-confirmed on Pythia-410M hierarchical). No alpha tuning. No held-out calibration. Just remove what was opposing you.

canceller ablation · 6 main cells · paired BCa 95% CI

The same population structure is not useful for additive steering. Building a single steering vector from the writer mean — the analogue of Todd's FV with cancellers removed — produces a larger absolute held-out logit shift in 6 / 6 cells, but the direction is task-conditional and the partition effect on accuracy is not established under norm-matched injection. Population-level identification tells you which heads to intervene on; it doesn't tell you that aggregated steering vectors are the right tool to clean up per-prompt canceller behaviour.

downstream · ablation

Targeted canceller lesion

+0.13 → +0.29 nats correct-logit

Zero-ablate just the canceller subgroup at inference. Calibration-free; CI excludes zero in 6 / 6 main cells; directional +2 to +7 pp accuracy.

use when you have a frozen FV-candidate set and want a calibration-free read-out gain.

attribution · audit

Magnitude-only top-K is biased

64% / 8% canceller capture · hier / mod

Todd-style top-K surfaces the locally dominant role and silently drops the other. hier top-20 is 64% cancellers (4% writers); mod reverses to 59% writers (8% cancellers).

use when comparing FV inventories: pre-split, the same magnitude list means different things across tasks.

steering · caveat

Writer-mean steering doesn't transfer

6 / 6 · 0 / 6 logit ↑ · accuracy ↑

Writer-only vector beats FV-mean on absolute held-out logit shift in 6 / 6 cells but loses on transplant accuracy in 6 / 6. Population identification ≠ recipe for an aggregated steering vector.

avoid when: deploying additive steering for accuracy. The win is per-prompt ablation, not a vector.

Ablation works because cancellers are a population — not because cancellers can be summarised by a vector.
